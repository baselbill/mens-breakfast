Our guiding principles

Five things that shape
how we gather.

We're a group of men who meet weekly to study Scripture, share life, and pray together. These principles keep our time honest, focused, and rooted in the Word.

I · Principle One

Scripture-Centered Discussion

What is the primary focus of our breakfast discussions?

To grow in our understanding of God through Christ-centered conversation, recognizing that Scripture is a deep well always worth exploring together. We seek not only to understand God's Word but to be shaped by it — in our character, leadership, families, and daily lives.

Psalm 119:105

Why do we prioritize discussing God over other topics?

Our main focus is growing in the knowledge of God through His Word, since a right understanding of Scripture shapes how we see everything else. Other topics like politics or current events may come up, but we discuss through a biblical lens and avoid debates that distract from spiritual growth.

2 Peter 3:18

How should we approach different perspectives within our group?

We welcome different perspectives on the faith, sharpening one another like iron sharpens iron, but we look to Scripture as our shared foundation and final guide in every discussion.

Proverbs 27:17
II · Principle Two

Confidentiality

What makes this group a safe space for men?

We gather as brothers in Christ to talk openly about life's struggles and joys. In a world where men are often expected to appear strong and self-sufficient, this is a place to be honest, vulnerable, and supported.

Psalm 55:22

How do we ensure that trust is preserved?

We keep personal stories and struggles confidential. What is shared within the group stays within the group, out of respect and love for one another.

Proverbs 17:17
III · Principle Three

Teaching vs Facilitation

Who is the teacher in our small group?

The Word of God alone, preserved in the Bible, is our teacher. No man among us holds the position of teacher — but we learn from each other.

John 14:26

What is the role of a facilitator, and how does it differ from a teacher?

The facilitator keeps the conversation centered on Scripture, ensures time is well managed, and makes space for everyone to contribute — guiding discussion and prompting reflection rather than instructing or teaching. He doesn't come to teach but to serve the group by pointing everyone back to the Word of God and keeping conversations centered on Christ, believing we can learn from one another as Scripture leads.

2 Corinthians 1:24
IV · Principle Four

Praying Together

How do we pray together?

We conclude each gathering with a time of shared prayer requests. Anyone may pray openly, follow along silently, or lift their prayers quietly before God.

Matthew 18:19—20

Why do we value prayer?

Prayer unites us in spirit and purpose. As we lift one another's burdens before God, we strengthen our brotherhood and grow together in faith.

James 5:16
V · Principle Five

Simplicity in Gathering

When and where do we meet?

We meet at Crossroads Basel every Saturday at 7:30 AM. Discussion begins at 8:00, and prayer concludes at 9:00 AM. Our schedule usually does not change throughout the seasons or holidays — if there's ever a change in time or location, it will be notified in advance.

Why do we keep our schedule and format simple?

Because consistency fosters faithfulness. Wherever two or three are gathered, the Holy Spirit is with us, and keeping things simple removes barriers to fellowship.

What should I bring for breakfast?

Bring whatever you wish to share, without pressure or expectation. There is no need to impress — only to participate joyfully.

Why do we keep breakfast simple?

To maintain focus on fellowship rather than food. The goal is gathering together in Christ, not what's on the table.
